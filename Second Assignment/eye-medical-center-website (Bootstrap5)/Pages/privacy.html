Privacy Policy

Your privacy and the security of your personal information are of utmost importance to us.

Last Updated: January 1, 2025

1. Introduction

Eye Medical Center ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our facility, use our services, or interact with our website.

We comply with all applicable federal and state privacy laws, including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Name, address, phone number, and email address
  • Date of birth and Social Security number
  • Insurance information and payment details
  • Emergency contact information
  • Employment information (when relevant to treatment)

2.2 Health Information

We collect health information necessary for providing eye care services, including:

  • Medical history and current health conditions
  • Eye examination results and diagnostic test results
  • Treatment plans and medication information
  • Surgical records and follow-up care notes
  • Images and photographs of your eyes (when medically necessary)

2.3 Website Information

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect:

  • IP address and browser information
  • Pages visited and time spent on our site
  • Referring website information
  • Device and operating system information

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Treatment

We use your health information to:

  • Provide, coordinate, and manage your eye care treatment
  • Consult with other healthcare providers involved in your care
  • Refer you to specialists when necessary
  • Provide follow-up care and monitor your progress

3.2 Payment

We use your information to:

  • Process insurance claims and obtain payment for services
  • Verify insurance coverage and benefits
  • Collect payment for services rendered
  • Respond to payment-related inquiries

3.3 Healthcare Operations

We may use your information for:

  • Quality assessment and improvement activities
  • Staff training and education
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Business planning and development

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 Authorized Disclosures

We may share your information with:

  • Healthcare providers involved in your treatment
  • Insurance companies for payment and coverage verification
  • Family members or friends you have authorized
  • Business associates who assist with our operations

4.2 Required Disclosures

We may be required to disclose your information:

  • To you or your authorized representative
  • To the Department of Health and Human Services for compliance investigations
  • When required by law or court order
  • For public health activities
  • To prevent serious threats to health or safety

4.3 Marketing and Communications

We may contact you for:

  • Appointment reminders and follow-up care
  • Treatment alternatives and health-related benefits
  • Educational materials about eye health
  • Practice newsletters (with your consent)

5. Your Rights

5.1 Access to Your Information

You have the right to:

  • Request and receive a copy of your medical records
  • Inspect your health information
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information

5.2 Control Over Your Information

You have the right to:

  • Request restrictions on how we use or disclose your information
  • Request confidential communications
  • Revoke authorizations (except where action has already been taken)
  • File a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

5.3 Notification of Breaches

We will notify you if there is a breach of your unsecured health information that may compromise the privacy or security of your information.

6. Information Security

6.1 Physical Safeguards

  • Secure facilities with controlled access
  • Locked filing cabinets for paper records
  • Secure disposal of confidential information

6.2 Technical Safeguards

  • Encrypted electronic health records systems
  • Secure network connections and firewalls
  • Regular security updates and monitoring
  • Access controls and user authentication

6.3 Administrative Safeguards

  • Staff training on privacy and security policies
  • Regular risk assessments and audits
  • Incident response procedures
  • Business associate agreements

7. Website Privacy

7.1 Cookies and Tracking

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Improve website functionality and user experience
  • Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
  • Remember your preferences and settings

7.2 Third-Party Services

Our website may include links to third-party websites or services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites.

7.3 Online Forms

Information submitted through our website contact forms is transmitted securely and handled according to this privacy policy.

8. Minors' Privacy

For patients under 18 years of age:

  • Parents or legal guardians may access their child's health information
  • We comply with state laws regarding adolescent privacy rights
  • Certain sensitive information may require additional consent

9. Changes to This Policy

We reserve the right to modify this Privacy Policy at any time. Changes will be effective immediately upon posting on our website. We will notify patients of significant changes through:

  • Posted notices in our facility
  • Updates on our website
  • Direct communication when required by law
