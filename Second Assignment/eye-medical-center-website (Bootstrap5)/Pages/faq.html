General Questions

The frequency of eye exams depends on your age, risk factors, and overall eye health:

  • Ages 18-39: Every 2-3 years if no risk factors
  • Ages 40-54: Every 1-2 years
  • Ages 55-64: Every 1-2 years
  • Ages 65+: Annually

If you have diabetes, high blood pressure, family history of eye disease, or other risk factors, more frequent exams may be recommended.

A comprehensive eye exam typically includes:

  • Visual acuity testing
  • Refraction assessment for glasses/contacts
  • Eye muscle movement evaluation
  • Peripheral vision testing
  • Intraocular pressure measurement
  • Dilated fundus examination
  • Digital retinal photography
  • OCT imaging (if indicated)

The entire exam usually takes 60-90 minutes.

We primarily operate by appointment to ensure adequate time for each patient. However, we do accommodate urgent eye care needs and emergencies. For non-urgent care, we recommend scheduling an appointment to avoid wait times.

Ophthalmologists are medical doctors who specialize in comprehensive eye care, including medical and surgical treatment of eye diseases.

Optometrists are healthcare professionals who provide primary eye care, including eye exams, vision correction, and treatment of certain eye conditions.

Our center has both ophthalmologists and optometrists working together to provide comprehensive care.

Appointments

You can schedule an appointment by:

  • Calling our office at (123) 456-7890
  • Using our online contact form
  • Visiting our office in person

Our scheduling team will help you find the most convenient time and ensure you see the appropriate specialist for your needs.

Please bring:

  • Valid photo ID
  • Insurance cards
  • Current glasses and/or contact lenses
  • List of current medications
  • Previous eye exam records (if available)
  • Sunglasses for after dilation

Appointment times vary depending on the type of visit:

  • Comprehensive eye exam: 60-90 minutes
  • Follow-up visit: 30-45 minutes
  • Contact lens fitting: 45-60 minutes
  • Surgical consultation: 45-60 minutes

We'll inform you of the expected duration when scheduling your appointment.

We require at least 24 hours notice for appointment cancellations or rescheduling. This allows us to offer the time slot to other patients who may need care. Late cancellations or no-shows may result in a fee.

Surgery

LASIK candidacy depends on several factors:

  • Age (typically 18+ years)
  • Stable prescription for at least one year
  • Adequate corneal thickness
  • No active eye diseases
  • Realistic expectations

A comprehensive evaluation with our refractive surgery specialist will determine if LASIK is right for you.

The actual cataract surgery typically takes 15-20 minutes per eye. However, plan for 2-3 hours at our facility for pre-operative preparation, the procedure, and initial recovery. Most patients go home the same day.

Recovery times vary by procedure:

  • LASIK: 24-48 hours for basic activities, 1-2 weeks for full recovery
  • Cataract surgery: 1-2 days for basic activities, 4-6 weeks for complete healing
  • Retinal surgery: Several weeks to months depending on complexity
  • Glaucoma surgery: 2-4 weeks for initial healing

Your surgeon will provide specific recovery instructions based on your procedure.

Yes, you will need someone to drive you home after any eye surgery. Your vision may be temporarily blurred, and you may have received sedation. We recommend arranging for transportation both to and from your surgery appointment.

Pediatric Care

We recommend eye exams at these ages:

  • 6 months: First comprehensive eye exam
  • 3 years: Second comprehensive exam
  • Before kindergarten: Pre-school vision screening
  • Annually: Throughout school years

Earlier exams may be needed if there are concerns about vision development or family history of eye problems.

We use age-appropriate testing methods:

  • Picture charts instead of letters
  • Objective testing that doesn't require verbal responses
  • Specialized pediatric equipment
  • Games and activities to keep children engaged

Our pediatric specialists are trained to work with children of all ages and developmental levels.

Watch for these warning signs:

  • Frequent eye rubbing or blinking
  • Covering or closing one eye
  • Tilting head to one side
  • Difficulty with reading or schoolwork
  • Sitting too close to TV or holding books very close
  • Complaints of headaches or tired eyes
  • Eyes that appear crossed or don't move together

If you notice any of these signs, schedule an appointment promptly.
